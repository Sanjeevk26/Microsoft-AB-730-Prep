# Verification Steps for Microsoft 365 Copilot Responses

## 1. Overview

When using Microsoft 365 Copilot, responses should not be accepted without validation. Verification ensures that outputs are:

- Accurate
- Reliable
- Safe to use in decision-making

The level of verification should be appropriate to the task.

---

## 2. Task-Based Verification

Verification effort should depend on:

- Sensitivity of the data
- Impact of the decision
- Use case (informational vs business-critical)

### Key Principle

- Do not over-verify simple queries
- Apply deeper verification for high-risk or critical tasks

---

## 3. Example Scenario

Prompt:
"What is the exchange rate between Japanese yen and the euro?"

Copilot provides a response.

### Required Action

- Do not rely solely on the response
- Perform verification steps based on importance

---

## 4. Checking Citations

Copilot may provide sources for its responses.

### Steps

1. Click on **Sources**
2. Review referenced links or documents
3. Validate:
   - Authenticity of sources
   - Relevance to the query

---

### Risk

- AI may generate fabricated or incorrect citations
- Real-world cases exist where incorrect citations caused serious consequences

---

## 5. Validating the Response

In addition to checking sources:

- Review the accuracy of the answer
- Cross-check with trusted sources
- Ensure the output aligns with expectations

---

### Ask Follow-up Questions

If needed, ask Copilot:

- "How did you calculate this?"
- "Provide step-by-step reasoning"
- "Explain your sources"

---

## 6. Human Review

Human oversight is critical:

- Validate outputs before acting on them
- Especially important for:
  - Financial decisions
  - Legal content
  - Business presentations

---

## 7. Microsoft AI Principles for Validation

Responses should be evaluated against the following principles:

---

### 7.1 Fairness

- AI should treat all individuals and groups equally
- No bias or discrimination between similar groups

---

### 7.2 Reliability and Safety

- AI should function as intended
- Should resist harmful or unintended manipulation
- Should respond safely to unexpected inputs

---

### 7.3 Privacy and Security

- Data must comply with privacy regulations
- Users should understand how their data is used
- Data must be:
  - Protected from unauthorized access
  - Encrypted in transit and at rest

---

### 7.4 Inclusiveness

- AI should treat individuals equally regardless of:
  - Gender
  - Ethnicity
  - Age
  - Financial background
  - Qualifications

---

### 7.5 Transparency

- AI decisions should be explainable
- Users should understand how outputs are generated

Example:
Ask Copilot to explain how it derived the result

---

### 7.6 Accountability

- AI system designers are responsible for system behavior
- Users are accountable for how outputs are used

Important:
You are responsible for decisions made using AI outputs

---

## 8. Best Practices

- Always verify important responses
- Check both sources and reasoning
- Use follow-up questions for clarity
- Apply human judgment before taking action
- Adjust verification depth based on risk
